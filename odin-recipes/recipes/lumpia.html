Lumpia

Description

Lumpia is a Filipino egg roll that is filled with a mixture of ground pork and chopped vegetables. It is typically served as an appetizer or snack and is often accompanied by a sweet and sour dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled, deveined, and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • 1 cup carrots, minced
  • 2 cups cabbage, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pack of lumpia wrapper

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, shrimp, onion, carrots, cabbage, egg, soy sauce, black pepper, and salt. Mix until well combined.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the meat and vegetable mixture to the skillet and cook until the pork is no longer pink.
  4. Remove the skillet from the heat and let the mixture cool.
  5. Place a lumpia wrapper on a clean surface and spoon about 2 tablespoons of the meat and vegetable mixture onto the wrapper.
  6. Roll the wrapper tightly around the filling, folding in the sides as you go.
  7. Seal the edge of the wrapper with a little water to secure the lumpia.
  8. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  9. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large skillet over medium heat.
  10. Fry the lumpia in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  11. Remove the lumpia from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  12. Serve the lumpia hot with sweet and sour dipping sauce.
